Government Subsidy — What's Actually Available

Under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana (launched Feb 2024), residential consumers can claim up to ₹78,000 subsidy for systems up to 3 kW, and proportionate amounts for larger systems. The subsidy is credited directly to your bank account after DISCOM approval. We assist with the complete portal registration and documentation — this is a legitimate central government scheme.

☁️

How Solar Performs on Cloudy & Rainy Days

Solar panels work on diffuse light, not just direct sunlight. On overcast days in North India, a well-installed system typically generates 40–60% of its peak output. Haryana and Delhi receive an average of 4.5–5.5 peak sun hours per day annually (per IMD data), which is considered high and makes rooftop solar economically viable year-round.

🔧

Warranties: What They Cover & What They Don't

Tier-1 solar panels carry a 25-year performance warranty guaranteeing at least 80% output at end of life. Inverters come with 5–10 year warranties depending on the brand. Our AMC covers routine maintenance and cleaning. Physical damage from extreme weather or external causes is typically covered under your home insurance — not the panel warranty.

📐

Not Every Roof Is Ideal — Here's What Matters

Solar works best on south- or west-facing roofs with minimal shading between 9am–4pm. Concrete RCC rooftops are the most suitable. Older roofs, asbestos sheets, or heavily shaded rooftops may require structural work or may not be suitable at all. Our engineer will assess this honestly during the free site survey — we won't recommend a system if your roof isn't a good fit.

📋

Net Metering — Selling Power Back to the Grid

Haryana (DHBVN/UHBVN) and Delhi (BSES/BYPL/TPDDL) both support net metering for rooftop solar. Excess units you generate are fed to the grid and credited on your next bill. The process takes 15–30 days for meter installation post-commissioning. We handle the application, but final approval timelines depend on your DISCOM.

⏱️

Realistic Installation Timeline

A typical residential system (3–6 kW) takes 1–2 working days for physical installation. However, the complete process from inquiry to first electricity generation — including site survey, design, material procurement, installation, and net meter application — usually takes 2–4 weeks. We'll give you a clear schedule before any work begins.
